Do you find the game challenging? Excellent! It's intentionally designed to be difficult, aiming to inspire reflection on the utility of various summary measures for understanding the diverse living conditions across the globe. This challenge is not just about guessing, but about engaging with the data that paints a picture of each country's unique circumstances.

After two incorrect guesses, you'll receive a hint to help you along. Additionally, if you're unsure about any of the indicators listed, brief summaries are provided below for your convenience. Dive in, test your knowledge, and learn more about the world's countries through their key statistics.

Total fertility rate
The average number of children a woman would have if she experienced current age-specific fertility rates throughout her reproductive life. The replacement level is approximately 2.1 children per woman, which in the long run is needed to sustain the population size.
Life expectancy
The average number of years a newborn is expected to live, assuming current mortality rates remain constant throughout their life.
Sex ratio
The number of males for every 100 females in a population â€“ most notably differs across populations due to gender differences in mortality and migration.
Density
Population density measures how many people live in each square kilometer of a given area, indicating the concentration of human settlement.
GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
The total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders on a yearly basis.
GDP per capita
The GDP of a country divided by its population, representing the average income or economic output per person.
Relative poverty rate
The percentage of the population earning less than 50% of the median income.
Gini-index
The Gini index measures income inequality within a population, ranging from 0 (perfect equality) to 100 (perfect inequality).

Welcome to our interactive country guessing game! This educational tool challenges you to guess the country based on various demographic, economic, and social indicators, making learning about different nations both fun and engaging.

The data used in this game is sourced from the World Bank's extensive databank, ensuring accuracy and up-to-date information. The game includes data for over 200 countries, reflecting the most recent estimates available from the World Bank, which is continuously updated to provide the latest statistics.

By playing this game, you will test your knowledge and gain insights into the socio-economic conditions of different countries. Each hint is based on real data, helping you make informed guesses and learn in the process.

We hope you enjoy the game and find it both fun and educational. For more detailed information and to explore the full range of data provided by the World Bank, please visit the World Bank's databank.
